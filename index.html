Meet the Fair Core License

Fair Core is to Fair Source what Open Core is to Open Source.

A perfect blend of the Functional Source License 1.1 & the Elastic License 2.0 balancing developer sustainability & user freedom. Safely share projects with commercial features under a single codebase, while eventually contributing to Open Source.

The pitch

The Fair Core License, or FCL, is a mostly-permissive non-compete Fair Source license that eventually changes to Open Source.

The FAQ

How is the FCL different than the FSL?

The FSL by design offers no protection for commercial features, i.e. somebody could fork your project to give themselves the commercial features without recourse. The only way around that is to separately license the commercial features, but then those features will never become Open Source.

In contrast, the FCL allows you to safely share all your code under a singe codebase & license, & eventually contribute all of it to Open Source.

How is the FCL different than the ELv2?

The ELv2 fails to protect users' long-term access to commercial features if the licensor shuts down & is unable to renew license keys, i.e. after a user's license key expires, their access to the commercial features is lost forever.

In contrast, the FCL safeguards a user's long-term access by eventually converting to an Open Source license. We wrote about our journey relicensing from ELv2.

How are commercial features safely shared?

Your project's commercial features can be gated with a license key. The FCL includes provisions that protect those features from unauthorized access. Instead of separately licensing your commercial features, e.g. under an ee/ directory, they can all live under the same codebase & license.

What types of projects are a good fit for FCL?

Open Core or self-hosted projects with commercial features that want to securely share all of their code, not just some of it.

How does the FCL contribute to Open Source?

Like the FSL, projects licensed under the FCL will change over to an Open Source license after 2 years, balancing developer sustainability & user freedom.

What Open Source licenses are supported?

Like the FSL, the FCL supports Apache 2.0 & MIT change licenses.

Why only Apache 2.0 and MIT?

We're following what the FSL chooses to support.

Who's using the FCL?

Right now, it's used by us, Keygen, but we'd love it if other projects joined us.
